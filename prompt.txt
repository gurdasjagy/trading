You have just crossed the threshold from retail algorithmic trading into true institutional quantitative finance. Realizing the difference between Futures-Futures and Spot-Futures is the exact "aha" moment that separates bots that bleed money from bots that print steady yield.

When you go Long on Futures A and Short on Futures B, you are exposed to **basis risk**. The mark prices on two different exchanges can diverge wildly during high volatility, causing floating losses. Worse, you can get liquidated on *both* sides if the divergence is sharp enough. 

In **Spot-Futures Arbitrage** (also known as Cash and Carry), you buy the actual underlying asset in the Spot market and Short the exact same amount in the Perpetual Futures market. 
* Your Spot position **cannot be liquidated**—you own the asset. 
* If the price drops 50%, your Spot loses 50% value, but your Short is in 50% profit. Net = $0.
* Your pure profit comes from collecting the **Funding Rate** continuously, because Perps usually trade at a premium to Spot, meaning Shorts get paid to hold their position.

Because this involves real capital across Gate.io, Bybit, and Binance, here is the exact, end-to-end blueprint for building an institutional-grade Spot-Perp arbitrage engine. No hardcoded values. Full real-time dynamics.

---

### Phase 1: The Ingestion Layer (Dynamic Initialization)
Institutional bots do not use hardcoded string symbols or fixed contract sizes. Everything is pulled from the exchange dynamically at startup and refreshed via WebSockets.

**1. Exchange Specifications (REST API Bootstrapping)**
Before opening a single WebSocket, your bot must query the exchange endpoints (e.g., Binance `/fapi/v1/exchangeInfo`, Bybit `/v5/market/instruments-info`) to build an internal memory map of:
* `baseAsset` and `quoteAsset` (e.g., BTC and USDT).
* `contractMultiplier`: Does 1 contract = 1 BTC, or 0.0001 BTC, or $1 USD? 
* `tickSize`: The minimum price movement (e.g., $0.10). You cannot place an order for $65,000.155.
* `stepSize` / `minOrderQty`: The minimum amount you can buy/short.
* `feeTiers`: Your exact Maker and Taker fee percentages.

**2. The Real-Time WebSocket Feeds**
Your bot must maintain highly optimized, asynchronous WebSocket connections for:
* **L2 Orderbook (Best Bid / Best Ask):** Do not pull the whole book. You only need the top 5 levels to calculate slippage and spread.
* **Mark Price & Index Price:** Used to calculate liquidation risk.
* **Live Funding Rate & Countdown:** Funding is not always 8 hours anymore. Bybit and Binance have dynamic funding that can settle every 4 hours or even every 1 hour depending on volatility. Gate.io often uses 4-8 hours. Your bot must track the exact milliseconds until the next settlement epoch.

---

### Phase 2: The Opportunity Scanner (Yield vs. Cost Math)
Your engine constantly scans pairs across Spot and Futures. To open a position, the projected yield must absolutely crush the execution costs.

**The Real-Time Math (Calculated on every orderbook tick):**
1.  **Projected Funding APR:** `(Live Funding Rate * (24 / Epoch Hours)) * 365`
    *Example:* If funding is 0.05% every 8 hours -> `(0.0005 * 3) * 365 = 54.75% APR`.
2.  **Spread Cost:** You are buying Spot at the Ask and Shorting Perps at the Bid. 
    `Spread % = (Perp Bid Price - Spot Ask Price) / Spot Ask Price`
3.  **Round-Trip Fee Cost:** `Total Fees = (Spot Entry Fee + Perp Entry Fee) + (Spot Exit Fee + Perp Exit Fee)`
    *Note:* If you use Taker (Market) orders, you pay ~0.04% to 0.06% per leg. That's up to 0.24% round trip, which might wipe out a whole week of funding profits!

**The Entry Trigger:**
The bot only signals an entry if: `(Expected Funding Yield over X days) > (Spread Cost + Total Fees + Slippage)`.

---

### Phase 3: Capital Allocation & Dynamic Sizing
Since you are trading Spot, you cannot use high leverage. Spot requires 1x capital. 

**The Institutional Allocation Model (Unified Margin):**
Assume you have $10,000 total capital.
* If you put $5,000 in Spot to buy BTC, you have $5,000 in Futures to short BTC. 
* If you Short at 1x leverage, your Short liquidation price is roughly 2x the current price (very safe).
* **Capital Efficiency (Advanced):** Institutions use 2x or 3x leverage on the short side. They put $7,500 in Spot and $2,500 in Futures at 3x leverage. This increases total yield, *but* requires a dynamic margin rebalancer (explained in Phase 5).

**Calculating the Exact Sizes:**
1.  Query available USDT balance in Spot and Futures wallets dynamically.
2.  Calculate maximum affordable Notional value.
3.  Divide by `Spot Ask Price` to get `Raw Crypto Quantity`.
4.  Floor the quantity using the `stepSize` of *both* the Spot and Futures markets.
    *Example:* If Spot allows 0.0001 step, but Futures requires 0.001 step, your final order size MUST be a multiple of 0.001, otherwise your hedge will be lopsided and exposed to market movement.

---

### Phase 4: Execution Engine (Defeating Legging Risk)
This is where 99% of retail bots fail. You cannot just send two Market orders simultaneously. Network latency guarantees one will fill at a worse price, destroying your profit margin.

**The "Maker-Taker Sniper" Algorithm:**
To maximize profit, you want to pay Maker fees (or get rebates) on at least one side.
1.  **Identify the Illiquid Leg:** Check the orderbook depth. Usually, the Spot market has thinner liquidity than the Perp market.
2.  **Place Maker Order:** Send a Limit `Post-Only` order to the Spot market right at the Best Bid (or 1 tick above it). 
3.  **Wait for the Fill (WebSocket User Data Stream):** Your bot listens to the private execution stream. 
4.  **The Instant Hedge (Taker):** The *millisecond* the Spot order partially or fully fills, the bot fires a Market (or aggressive Limit IOC - Immediate or Cancel) order to the Futures market for the exact filled amount. 

*Why this works:* You capture the spread and pay zero (or negative) fees on the Spot leg, and immediately lock in the delta-neutral hedge on the highly liquid Perp leg.

---

### Phase 5: Live Tracking & The Survival Layer (Risk Management)
Once the trade is open, the bot shifts into "Monitoring Mode." It must track the following metrics tick-by-tick:

**1. Dynamic Margin Rebalancing (Crucial for Real Money)**
If you are shorting BTC at 2x leverage, and BTC pumps 40% in a week, your Spot portfolio is highly profitable, but your Futures account is bleeding margin and approaching liquidation. 
* **The Fix:** Your bot must monitor the `marginRatio` of the Futures account. If it drops below a safe threshold (e.g., 80% to liquidation), the bot automatically executes an internal transfer via API, moving USDT profits from the Spot wallet into the Futures wallet to push the liquidation price infinitely far away. Binance and Bybit's Unified Trading Accounts handle some of this natively, but Gate.io requires strict manual API transfers.

**2. True PnL Tracking**
The bot calculates live PnL not by the price of the asset (which doesn't matter since you are hedged), but by:
`Live PnL = Accumulated Realized Funding + (Current Perp Bid - Entry Perp Price) + (Current Spot Bid - Entry Spot Price) - Fees Paid`

---

### Phase 6: The Unwind (Exiting the Position)
You don't hold the position forever. You must close the loop when the math changes.

**Exit Triggers:**
1.  **Negative Funding:** If the market crashes and Perps start trading at a discount to Spot, funding turns negative. Shorts start *paying* Longs. The bot must instantly detect this in the next epoch projection and unwind the position.
2.  **Basis Reversion / Premium Collapse:** If you entered when Perps were $100 higher than Spot, and suddenly they are equal, you can close the position to capture that $100 spread as an immediate capital gain on top of the funding you earned.

**Unwind Execution:**
The exact reverse of Phase 4. You are holding Spot BTC and a Short Perp. 
Place a Limit order to buy back the Short (closing it). The millisecond it fills, market-sell the Spot BTC.
